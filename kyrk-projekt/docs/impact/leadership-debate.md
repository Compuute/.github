# Module: Leadership & Debate

## 1. Purpose

Build critical thinking, public speaking, and group-leadership skills
through structured debate and small-team leadership exercises. Gives teens
and young adults a safe space to practice being heard, disagreeing well,
and leading with care.

## 2. Target group

- **Age band(s)**: `13-17`, `18-25`
- **Participant profile**: motivated teens and young adults; prior Youth
  Tech graduates welcome
- **Barrier considerations**: free; topics chosen to include newcomers to
  Swedish; confidentiality agreement at the start of every cohort

## 3. Activity patterns

- **Frequency**: biweekly cohort of 8 weeks
- **Duration**: 120 minutes per session
- **Format**: cohort-based, structured: debate → reflection → leadership
  exercise
- **Facilitators**: 1 lead facilitator + 1 co-facilitator (ideally an alumna)
- **Equipment / space**: discussion room, flip charts, timer

## 4. KPI fields

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| `activity_type` | `leadership` (for leadership exercises) or `debate` (for debate sessions) |
| `date` | per session |
| `location` | church address |
| `funding_tag` | `arvsfonden` |
| `participants_total` | cohort headcount |
| `age_band_counts` | `{13-17, 18-25}` |

## 5. ROI drivers

- **Cost per participant**: target 700–1000 SEK / participant / cohort
- **Grant leverage ratio**: target 2.0
- **Continuity**: 2 cohorts / year (spring + autumn)
- **Multiplier**: alumni return as co-facilitators → sustainability loop

## 6. Grant relevance

- **Arvsfonden** — democratic participation and youth leadership
- **Allmänna Arvsfonden** — civic engagement, integration

**Evidence required**: cohort size, completion rate, alumni return rate.

## 7. Yearly reusability plan

- **Year 1**: 1 spring cohort, 1 autumn cohort
- **Year 2**: add a showcase debate event open to parents
- **Year 3**: launch peer-mentoring where Year 2 alumni lead Year 3 cohorts
- **Retirement criteria**: < 6 completions per cohort for 2 consecutive cohorts
