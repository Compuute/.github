# Module: Youth Tech

## 1. Purpose

Give teens a free, church-hosted space to build things with technology —
simple electronics, robotics kits, creative coding, and conversations about
digital ethics. Lowers the barrier for youth without a computer at home.
Feeds into the leadership cohort.

## 2. Target group

- **Age band(s)**: `13-17`, `18-25`
- **Participant profile**: teens and young adults with varying tech exposure
- **Barrier considerations**: no hardware required at home; all equipment
  provided on-site; Swedish + English welcome.

## 3. Activity patterns

- **Frequency**: weekly
- **Duration**: 90 minutes
- **Format**: drop-in workshop + a monthly showcase
- **Facilitators**: 2 volunteers, at least one with prior coding or maker
  experience
- **Equipment / space**: one room, 6 laptops, 2 robotics kits, electronics
  breadboards, power

## 4. KPI fields

| Field | Value |

| `activity_type` | `youth_tech` |

| `funding_tag` | `arvsfonden` |

| `age_band_counts` | `{13-17, 18-25}` primarily |

## 5. ROI drivers

- **Cost per participant**: target 800–1200 SEK / participant / month
- **Grant leverage ratio**: target 2.0 (2 SEK grant per 1 SEK own contribution)
- **Continuity**: 10 months / year (school calendar aligned)
- **Multiplier**: graduates feed `leadership-debate` cohort

## 6. Grant relevance

- **Arvsfonden** — youth development and digital inclusion criteria
- **Allmänna Arvsfonden** — innovation and experimentation in youth work
- **Kommunala bidrag** — local civic integration where offered

**Evidence required**: weekly participant counts, age-band breakdown,
showcase attendance.

## 7. Yearly reusability plan

- **Year 1**: establish cadence, prove participant counts
- **Year 2**: add a monthly "ethics salon" (discussion hour)
- **Year 3**: launch a teen-led mini-course where graduates co-facilitate
- **Retirement criteria**: < 5 participants per session for 2 consecutive months
